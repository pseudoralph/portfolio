On coming to Epicodus

It once was that the only path to programming was through a college or a university.

That’s precisely what I set out to do some 18 years ago. Unfortunately, youth is wasted on the young and I was too foolish to put in the effort required for a CS degree.

But much as changed in all those years.

For one thing, abstraction has both simplified and made more robust, the task of modern programming. And so too, has it simplified the path to programming.

Abstraction, simply put, allows oneself to build off of programmers’ past creations. Instead of going through the effort of writing a mapping program, you can simply make an API call to Google Maps and move along. Instead of digitizing a Zagat’s guide, you can make an API call to Yelp and quickly be on your way to that 4-star Vegan-friendly restaurant.

In today’s world of APIs, a programmers job can simply be putting all those pieces together.

That’s precisely what I’m setting off to do.

Better late than never, I suppose.
